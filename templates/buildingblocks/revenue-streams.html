Building Block

Description of Revenue Streams

The Revenue Streams building block defines the way in which a company generates income from its Customers. To create a sustainable and profitable business, it is important to identify what customers are willing to pay for and to develop a pricing strategy that matches the value proposition offered.

Forms of Revenue Streams: Revenue Streams can take different forms:
Asset sales are the most used Revenue Stream and are based on selling a physical product. Usage fees are generated by the use of a particular service, with customers paying based on the number of minutes spent on the phone, the number of nights they stay at a hotel, or the number of rides they take on a ride-sharing service. Subscription fees are generated by selling continuous access to a service, such as gym memberships, music streaming services, or software-as-a-service. Lending, renting, and leasing create Revenue Streams by temporarily granting someone the exclusive right to use a particular asset for a fixed period in return for a fee. Licensing generates Revenue Streams by giving customers permission to use protected intellectual property in exchange for licensing fees. Brokerage fees result from intermediation services performed on behalf of two or more parties, such as credit card providers or real estate agents. Finally, advertising generates Revenue Streams by charging fees for advertising a particular product, service, or brand.

Pricing mechanisms: To maximize Revenue Streams, it is important to choose the right pricing mechanism for each Revenue Stream and to constantly evaluate and optimize the pricing strategy. This can involve experimenting with different pricing strategies, such as bundling or unbundling offerings, offering discounts, or implementing dynamic pricing. Additionally, companies must be aware of external factors that can impact Revenue Streams, such as changing market conditions, change in customer behavior, and technological advancements.

What to remember about Revenue Streams

When developing the Revenue Streams building block, consider the following factors:

  • Identify what customers are willing to pay for and develop a pricing strategy that reflects the value proposition offered.
  • Choose the right pricing mechanism for each Revenue Stream and constantly evaluate and optimize the pricing strategy.
  • Experiment with different pricing strategies and be aware of external factors that can impact Revenue Streams, such as changing market conditions, shifts in customer behavior, and technological advancements.
  • Continuously monitor Revenue Streams to ensure that they remain profitable and sustainable and be willing to adjust the strategy as needed.
  • Consider the potential for multiple Revenue Streams and identify opportunities to generate income from complementary offerings or services.

Trigger Questions for Revenue Streams

  • For what value are our customers really willing to pay?
  • For what do they currently pay?
  • How are they currently paying?
  • How would they prefer to pay?
  • How much does each Revenue Stream contribute to overall revenues?
