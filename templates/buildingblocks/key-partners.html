Description of the Key Partners

The Key Partnerships Building Block describes the network of suppliers and partners that a company relies on to make its business model work. Partnerships can be formed for many reasons, including optimizing business models, reducing risk, and acquiring resources. There are four different types of partnerships, including strategic alliances between non-competitors, coopetition between competitors, joint ventures to develop new businesses, and buyer-supplier relationships to assure reliable supplies.

What to remember about Key Partners

When developing the Key Partnerships Building Block, remember the following:

  • Identify the partners that are essential to the success of the business model.
  • Evaluate the benefits and risks of different types of partnerships. This includes assessing the potential advantages and disadvantages of forming partnerships with different types of partners, including competitors and suppliers.
  • Assess the capabilities and resources of potential partners. This involves evaluating the strengths and weaknesses of potential partners and determining their suitability for the partnership.
  • Develop clear and specific partnership agreements. This includes creating contracts or agreements that define the roles and responsibilities of each partnerp.
  • Monitor and manage partnerships to ensure success. This includes tracking the performance of partnerships and making adjustments as necessary to ensure that the partnerships are meeting their objectives.
  • Maintain flexibility in partnerships to adapt to changing circumstances.
