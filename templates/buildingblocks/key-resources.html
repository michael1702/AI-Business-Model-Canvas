Description of Key Resources

The Key Resources Building Block describes the most important assets needed to create and offer a Value Proposition, reach markets, maintain relationships with Customer Segments, and earn revenues. Key Resources can be physical, financial, intellectual, or human, and they can be owned or leased by the company or acquired from key partners. Depending on the type of business model, different Key Resources are required.

Physical assets, such as manufacturing facilities, buildings, vehicles, machines, systems, point-of-sale systems, and distribution networks, are vital to capital-intensive businesses, such as retailers like Walmart and Amazon.com. Intellectual resources, such as brands, proprietary knowledge, patents and copyrights, partnerships, and customer databases, are critical to the success of consumer goods companies like Nike and Sony, as well as technology firms like Microsoft and SAP. In contrast, human resources are particularly prominent in knowledge-intensive and creative industries, such as pharmaceutical componies. Some business models call for financial resources and/or financial guarantees, such as cash, lines of credit, or a stock option pool for hiring key employees, and companies may leverage financial resources to provide vendor financing.

What to remember about Key Resources

When filling in the Key Resources Building Block, it is important to remember the following:

  • Identify the most critical assets required to create and offer a Value Proposition, reach markets, maintain relationships with Customer Segments, and earn revenues.
  • Evaluate which Key Resources are unique or rare, and how they provide a competitive advantage.
  • Optimize Key Resources to maximize their value to the business model. This may involve assessing how Key Resources can be used more effectively to generate revenue or increase efficiency.
  • Protect or defend Key Resources against competition through patents, trademarks, or trade secrets.
  • Analyze the overall impact of Key Resources on the business model and how they contribute to the creation of value for Customer Segments and the generation of revenue.
