  1. 1

    Mobilize

    Collect first ideas and goals for the business model development.

    During the Mobilize phase, it's crucial to select suitable project team members and gather information. We suggest conducting preliminary tests of the basic business idea in this phase, but remember, it should only serve as a starting point, as people tend to overestimate the potential of initial business ideas and close themselves off to alternatives.

    It's also essential to establish the Business Model Canvas as the common language of the design process. Doing so will improve communication and make it easier to present and structure ideas effectively. We recommend continuously testing new ideas with people from different backgrounds, using methods like Kill/Thrill Sessions to collect reasons for the idea's failure and success.

  2. 2

    Understanding

    Research the context of the planned enterprise, considering potential customers and the market situation.

    In the Understanding phase, it's important to develop a good understanding of the business model's context. This involves analyzing the business model's environment through market research, customer engagement and feedback, expert interviews, and sketching out competitors' business models. A deep understanding of the customer group is crucial, and tools like the Empathy Map can be useful in creating customer profiles and adapting the business model accordingly.

  3. 3

    Design

    Define possible business models using the Business Model Canvas and select the most promising model.

    During the Design phase, new models should be continuously developed and tested with the BMC. We recommend questioning current business models and patterns during the ideation phase to develop groundbreaking models. Think through multiple business model options, such as by searching for different revenue streams and analyzing the value of multiple distribution channels.

  4. 4

    Implement

    Implement the selected business model in reality.

    After the final business model is drafted, implementation begins. Associated projects, milestones, legal structures, and budgets are defined, and management of uncertainties is particularly important. Monitor risk factors and develop a mechanism for rapid business model adaptation to market feedback.

  5. 5

    Manage

    Adapt the business model to market developments and optimize it.

    The last phase of the Business Model Design process is managing the business model. We suggest continuously evaluating and adapting the business model to market influences. Assemble a team from different areas of the company to evaluate the business model together. Proactive adaptation to market developments and management of a portfolio of business models is recommended, especially in light of the ever-shortening product life cycles.
