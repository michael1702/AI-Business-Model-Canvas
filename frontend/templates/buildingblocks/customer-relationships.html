Building Block

Description of the Customer Relationships

The Customer Relationships Building Block describes the types of relationships a company establishes with specific Customer Segments. It is important for a company to clarify the type of relationship it wants to establish with each Customer Segment, as these relationships deeply influence the overall customer experience. Relationships may be driven by motivations such as customer acquisition, customer retention, or boosting sales.

What to remember about Customer Relationships

When developing the Customer Relationships Building Block, consider the following:

  • Clarify the type of relationship you want to establish with each Customer Segment.
  • Keep the motivationds behind customer relationships in mind, such as customer acquisition, customer retention and boosting sales (upselling).
  • Distinguish between different categories of customer relationships that may co-exist in your company's relationship with a particular Customer Segment, such as personal assistance, dedicated personal assistance, self-service, automated services, communities, and co-creation.

Trigger Questions for Customer Relationships

  • What type of relationship does each of our Customer Segments expect us to establish and maintain with them?
  • Which ones have we established?
  • How costly are they?
  • How are they integrated with the rest of our business model?
